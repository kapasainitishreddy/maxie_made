# I'm Killing 3 of My SaaS Products — All

**Duration:** 34 seconds  
**Pattern:** Cliffhanger / Engagement Bait (shock claim → polling → reveal teaser)  
**Hook (first 3s):** "I'm about to kill three of my five SaaS products. Here's why — and I want you to vote on which one to keep."

## Shot list
1. [0:00-0:03] Hard cut. Creator sitting in the dark, one monitor behind them showing a list of 5 product names. Red X starts drawing itself through 3 of them. Text: "The cull."
2. [0:03-0:09] "Six months in. Five products shipped. Three of them are dead weight. I'm going to kill them this Friday. PharmaIP Radar, CloudFinOps Co-Pilot, AutoHedge Pro, QuantaLab, PegWatch — three of those names are going away."

## Why this works
Cliffhanger posts generate 4–8x the comment volume of standard posts because the request is concrete, low-friction, and opinionated. The "comment which to KEEP" framing is psychologically easier than "which to kill" — keeping is a positive act. Naming all 5 products in the script gives viewers a reason to advocate for their favorite, which compounds comment length. The "Friday reveal" creates a second-wave engagement event 2–3 days later, which the algorithm treats as a returning creator and boosts the original post's reach.
